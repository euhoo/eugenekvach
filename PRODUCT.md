## Platform

## Users

- Hiring managers, engineering leaders, founders, and product teams evaluating Eugene for Senior Frontend Engineering or Applied AI Engineering work.
- Visitors arriving from a role-specific resume, Telegram, a referral, or a direct link who need to understand Eugene's fit and evidence quickly.

## Product Purpose

Give a concise, credible view of Eugene's two connected engineering tracks and lead an interested visitor to the relevant proof or a direct conversation. The site complements role-specific resumes; it does not replace them.

## Positioning

Eugene is a product engineer who connects senior enterprise frontend practice with production Applied AI: from interfaces and architecture to LLM agents, tools, memory, quality loops, cost, and operations. The central AI case is a system around an LLM, not a thin API wrapper.

## Capabilities and Constraints

- Static-first and framework-free: semantic HTML, shared CSS, and only small amounts of vanilla JavaScript where interaction clearly improves the story.
- No npm, package manager, application framework, TypeScript, or build step unless Eugene explicitly reverses that decision.
- Content must be concise and evidence-based. Never invent experience, ownership, or metrics.
- Never expose secrets, private medical data, corporate source code, or confidential internal details.
- GitHub, PDFs, demos, and other private or unfinished materials stay unpublished until Eugene explicitly approves them.
- The two professional tracks must remain distinct enough for role-specific evaluation while clearly belonging to one engineering practice.

- Existing Russian copy, architecture diagrams, and a public portrait asset in the repository.

## Product Principles

1. Put proof before broad claims.
2. Make the visitor understand the role fit and strongest evidence within seconds.
3. Keep Applied AI and Frontend legible as separate paths within one coherent engineering story.
4. Prefer concise, scannable content over exhaustive biography.
5. Protect truth, privacy, and professional confidentiality.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG 2.2 AA for contrast, semantics, keyboard use, focus visibility, and responsive behavior.
- Preserve legibility across narrow mobile screens, zoom, and reduced-motion preferences.
- Treat fast loading and minimal client-side work as part of inclusive access.
